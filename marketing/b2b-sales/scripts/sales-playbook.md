# B2B Sales Playbook: White-Label SaaS for Travel Agencies

## Campaign Overview
**Goal:** Close 5 deals @ $499-$2,499/month = $2,495-12,495 MRR
**Target:** 200 Japan-focused travel agencies
**Strategy:** 3-email sequence → 20 demo calls → 5 closed deals

---

## Discovery Call Script (10-15 minutes)

### Pre-Call Checklist
- [ ] Research agency website and social media
- [ ] Note their specialization (luxury, family, adventure, cultural)
- [ ] Estimate annual trip volume from website/reviews
- [ ] Load white-label demo with their branding (agency name + logo color)
- [ ] Have proposal template ready with their company name

### Opening (2 minutes)

**You:** "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I saw on your website that [specific detail about their agency] - that's really impressive."

**You:** "Before I show you the demo, can I ask: what's your current process for helping clients plan their Japan trips? Walk me through from first inquiry to them getting on the plane."

**Listen for:**
- Time spent on planning (15+ hours = pain point)
- Client questions they answer repeatedly (shows need for self-service)
- Cancellations due to overwhelm (shows need for visualization)
- Lack of differentiation from competitors

### Problem Identification (3 minutes)

**You:** "Got it. So if I'm hearing you right, you're spending [X hours] per client just on itinerary planning, and clients are still confused about logistics?"

**You:** "What would it mean for your business if you could cut that planning time by 80% while also giving clients a better experience?"

**Let them talk.** This is where they sell themselves.

**You:** "And I'm curious - how are you currently differentiating [Agency Name] from the dozens of other Japan travel agencies? What makes someone choose you over a competitor?"

**Listen for:**
- "We offer personalized service" (vague - they need differentiation)
- "We've been doing this for X years" (not a differentiator)
- "We have local connections" (good - can enhance with tech)

### Solution Introduction (2 minutes)

**You:** "That makes sense. Let me show you something that agencies like yours are using to stand out. I'm going to share my screen - can you see it?"

**Share screen with white-label demo pre-configured with their branding**

**You:** "This is what your clients would see if you were using our platform. Notice the [Agency Name] branding throughout?"

**Demo walkthrough:**
1. **Branded header** - "This is YOUR logo and colors, not ours"
2. **Interactive map** - "Click on day 1... see how it shows walking directions? That's reducing client questions by 80%"
3. **Offline mode** - "This works without internet in Japan. No roaming fees. Clients LOVE this"
4. **Affiliate links** - "See this JR Pass booking button? That's a tracked affiliate link. You earn ~$28 commission per click"
5. **Trilingual toggle** - "English, Chinese, Japanese. Instant translation"

**You:** "Here's the best part: you can launch this for [Agency Name] in 24 hours. You upload your itinerary, customize the colors, and it goes live. No coding, no tech team."

### Objection Handling (3 minutes)

**Common Objections & Responses:**

**Objection:** "This seems expensive."
**Response:** "I get it. Let's do quick math. You're doing [X] trips per year. If this saves you 12 hours per trip at $50/hour value, that's [$X,XXX/year]. Plus affiliate revenue of ~[$X,XXX/year]. The platform pays for itself in month one."

**Objection:** "My clients aren't tech-savvy."
**Response:** "That's exactly why this works. It's simpler than Google Maps. They click a link, see their entire trip. No app download required. And you can still send them the PDF - this is just a bonus tool."

**Objection:** "I need to think about it."
**Response:** "Totally fair. Can I ask - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it budget, timing, or something about the product itself?" [Address the real objection]

**Objection:** "We're already using [other tool]."
**Response:** "What do you like about it? ... What's missing? [Listen] Our agencies switched from [tool] because [address their pain point]. Happy to show you a side-by-side comparison."

**Objection:** "I don't have time to set this up."
**Response:** "That's the beauty of white-label. You send us one sample itinerary and brand guidelines. We do the setup. You approve it. Goes live in 24 hours. After that, you can create new trips yourself in under 30 minutes."

### Value Proposition Reinforcement (2 minutes)

**You:** "Let me be super clear about what you're getting here:"

**For the agency:**
- Cut planning time from 15 hours to 3 hours per trip
- Generate $2,000-4,000/month in passive affiliate income
- Premium differentiator (charge 20% more than competitors)
- Reduce cancellations by 50% (clients see trip before booking)

**For their clients:**
- Branded mobile experience
- Works offline in Japan
- Turn-by-turn directions
- Live cherry blossom forecasts
- Zero learning curve

**You:** "The agencies winning right now are the ones offering a digital experience that justifies their service fee. DIY planners use ChatGPT. Budget travelers use Rome2Rio. Your clients? They get a white-glove digital companion that shows them you care about every detail."

### Closing (3 minutes)

**Soft Close:**
**You:** "Does this make sense for [Agency Name]? Can you see your clients using this?"

**If yes:**
**You:** "Great! Here's what I'm thinking. We're offering the first month free for agencies that sign up before April 1st. That gives you a full month to test it with real clients, zero risk. If it doesn't work, you cancel. If it does, you're paying [$X]/month and generating way more than that in affiliate revenue."

**You:** "I can get you set up this week. When's your next Japan trip booking?"

**If hesitant:**
**You:** "What would need to be true for this to be a no-brainer yes?"

**Address final concerns, then:**

**You:** "How about this - I'll send you a custom proposal with [Agency Name] branding, the exact pricing for your tier, and projected ROI based on your [X] annual trips. You review it with your team, and we hop on a quick call next week. Sound good?"

### Next Steps

**After Call:**
1. Send custom proposal within 2 hours (while you're fresh in their mind)
2. Include demo link with their branding
3. Add to CRM with stage: "Demo Completed - Proposal Sent"
4. Follow up in 3 days if no response
5. Follow up in 7 days with "breakup email" if still no response

## Negotiation Framework

### Pricing Flexibility

**Standard Tier ($499/mo):**
- Can offer: First month free (already standard)
- Cannot offer: Lower than $499/mo (breaks unit economics)

**Premium Tier ($999/mo):**
- Can offer: First 2 months free for annual contract
- Can offer: 10% discount for annual pre-pay ($10,788 vs $11,988)

**Enterprise Tier ($2,499/mo):**
- Can offer: First 3 months free for annual contract
- Can offer: 15% discount for annual pre-pay ($25,490 vs $29,988)
- Can offer: Custom integrations included

### When They Ask for Discounts

**Response:** "I totally get wanting the best deal. Here's what I can do: if you commit to an annual contract today, I'll give you [discount above]. That brings your effective monthly cost to [$X] instead of [$Y]. Does that work?"

**If they push harder:**
**Response:** "I appreciate you wanting to find a number that works. Here's my honest answer: we price this to be profitable at scale. At [$X], we can't deliver the service quality you expect. What I CAN do is prove value - take the first month free, use it with real clients, and if you don't see at least 2x ROI, we part ways. Fair?"

## Pre-Call Research Checklist

For every demo call, spend 10 minutes researching:

1. **Website Analysis:**
   - What's their specialization?
   - Do they mention "personalized service" or "custom itineraries"?
   - What's their price point (budget, mid, luxury)?
   - Do they have testimonials? What do clients praise?

2. **Social Media:**
   - Are they active on Instagram/Facebook?
   - Do they post client photos/reviews?
   - How many followers? (indicates scale)

3. **Reviews (Google/Yelp/TripAdvisor):**

   - What complaints appear repeatedly?
   - How responsive are they to reviews?

4. **Competitive Intel:**
   - Who are their local competitors?
   - What makes them different?
   - Are competitors using tech tools?

**Use this intel in your opening:** "I saw on your Instagram that you specialize in [X] - that's exactly what we're seeing work best with the platform."

## Red Flags (When to Walk Away)

Don't waste time on:
- Agencies doing fewer than 20 trips/year (too small)
- Agencies that want 50%+ discount (price shoppers, won't stick)
- Agencies that want "free trial for 6 months" (not serious)
- Agencies that ghost after 3+ follow-ups (not interested, move on)
- Agencies asking for custom features before signing (scope creep)

**Your time is valuable. Focus on the 80% who fit the ideal customer profile.**

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Best Fit:**
- 50-200 trips/year (Standard to Premium tier)
- Specializes in Japan (not general Asia)
- Charges premium prices ($3,000+ per trip)
- Tech-forward (mentions "digital" or "innovation" on website)
- Active on social media (shows they care about client experience)
- 5+ years in business (stable, can afford $499-999/mo)

**Great Fit (Enterprise):**
- 200+ trips/year
- Multiple agents/locations
- Corporate travel component
- Already using some tech tools
- Looking to scale operations

**Pass:**
- Budget travel agencies (price sensitive)
- General travel agencies (not Japan-focused)
- Solo operators doing <30 trips/year
- Non-responsive to emails/calls

### Trial Close
**You:** "If we could address [their main objection], would you be ready to move forward today?"

**You:** "How about this: take the first month completely free. Use it with 5 real clients. If it doesn't save you time and make you money, you cancel. If it does, you keep it. Zero risk. Sound fair?"

## Post-Close Onboarding

Once they sign:

1. **Welcome email** (immediate):
   - "Congrats on joining! Here's what happens next..."
   - Onboarding calendar invite
   - Brand guidelines request (logo, colors, sample itinerary)

2. **Onboarding call** (within 48 hours):
   - Walk through dashboard
   - Upload their first itinerary together
   - Customize branding
   - Test on mobile device
   - Answer questions

3. **First trip launch** (within 7 days):
   - Live link generated
   - Test with them on call
   - Teach them how to share with clients
   - Collect feedback

4. **30-day check-in**:
   - "How's it going? What do clients say?"
   - Upsell opportunities (upgrade tier if needed)
   - Request testimonial if positive

5. **90-day review**:
   - Show analytics (usage, affiliate revenue, time saved)
   - Ask for referrals to other agencies
   - Identify expansion opportunities
