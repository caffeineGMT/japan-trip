#### LinkedIn

**Post - Feb 5**
```
Lessons from building my first product in public:

I'm a software engineer by day, but I've spent the last 6 months building a
Japan trip planner on nights and weekends.

Why? I got lost in Kyoto and it ruined my day. Personal pain → Product idea.

What I've learned so far:

1. **Build for yourself first**
   If you wouldn't use it, why would anyone else?

2. **Talk to users early**
   I showed a broken prototype to 10 people. Their feedback shaped everything.

3. **Ship before you're ready**
   Perfectionism kills momentum. Launch, learn, iterate.

4. **Solve one problem really well**
   I could build 50 features. I'm building 5 that matter.

5. **Boring tech is fine**
   No fancy framework. Vanilla JS. Just works.

Launching on ProductHunt in April. Terrified and excited.

What's the best advice you'd give a first-time founder?
```

2. **Engage genuinely** - Actually respond to comments
3. **Show progress** - People love watching the journey
4. **Be vulnerable** - Share fears and anxieties
5. **Celebrate small wins** - Every signup matters
6. **Thank supporters** - Gratitude goes a long way

**Remember:** Social media is about building relationships, not broadcasting.
