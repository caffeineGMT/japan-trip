## The Problem

Planning a trip to Japan is overwhelming:
- **Time-consuming:** Average traveler spends 5+ days researching routes, restaurants, and attractions
- **Complex logistics:** Coordinating train schedules, opening hours, and cherry blossom bloom times
- **Language barrier:** Difficult to navigate without Japanese proficiency
- **Connectivity issues:** Expensive data roaming or unreliable WiFi
- **Information overload:** 20+ browser tabs, scattered notes, confusing spreadsheets

**The catalyst:** Founder got lost in Kyoto for an hour with a dead phone, no offline maps, and couldn't read Japanese signs. That frustration became the product.

---

## The Solution

**Japan Trip Companion** is an AI-powered Progressive Web App that simplifies Japan trip planning:

### Core Features

1. **AI Route Optimizer**
   - Analyzes 500+ destinations
   - Optimizes for cherry blossom season, opening hours, travel time
   - Creates perfect 2-week itinerary in 5 minutes

2. **100% Offline Capable**
   - Progressive Web App (PWA)
   - Service worker caching
   - Works in airplane mode
   - No data roaming charges

3. **Live Cherry Blossom Forecasts**
   - Pulls from 3 Japanese weather services
   - ML-powered bloom prediction
   - City-specific timing (Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Nara)

4. **Interactive Maps**
   - Walking & transit directions
   - Travel time estimates
   - Google Maps deep links
   - Leaflet.js powered

5. **Trilingual Support**
   - Toggle between English, Chinese (Simplified), Japanese
   - Place names, descriptions, key phrases
   - Instant switching

6. **Restaurant Reservations**
   - Confirmation numbers
   - Direct booking links
   - Japanese phrases for dining

7. **What's Next Mode**
   - Time-aware activity highlighting
   - Current location context
   - Smart notifications

8. **Weather Integration**
   - Daily forecasts
   - Per-city predictions
   - Travel planning context

## Origin Story

**Founder's Personal Experience:**

"It was April 2025. I was in Kyoto during peak cherry blossom season, trying to find Kinkaku-ji (the Golden Pavilion). Google Maps said 15 minutes away.

My phone died at 5%. I had no offline maps. I couldn't read the Japanese signs. I spent an hour wandering around, asking locals for directions with a language barrier, and nearly gave up.

When I finally found it, I was exhausted and frustrated. That night, I thought: 'There has to be a better way.'

Six months later, Japan Trip Companion was born. It's the tool I wish I had in that moment - offline maps, clear directions, Japanese phrases, and it just works."

## Technical Specifications

### Tech Stack

**Frontend:**
- Vanilla JavaScript (no framework, < 100KB bundle)
- Leaflet.js for interactive maps
- Service Workers for offline capability
- IndexedDB for local storage
- Progressive Web App (PWA)

**Backend:**
- Node.js + Express
- Supabase (PostgreSQL database)
- OpenAI GPT-4 for route optimization
- Stripe for payment processing

**Infrastructure:**
- Vercel Edge (CDN + serverless functions)
- Cloudflare DNS
- 99.9% uptime SLA

**APIs & Integrations:**
- OpenWeatherMap (weather data)
- Japan Meteorological Agency (cherry blossom forecasts)
- Google Maps API (directions fallback)

## FAQ for Journalists

**Q: Why build this when Google Maps exists?**
A: Google Maps is great for navigation, terrible for trip planning. It doesn't optimize routes, doesn't understand cherry blossom timing, and barely works offline. We're solving a different problem.

**Q: How is this different from TripAdvisor or Wanderlog?**
A: They're generic, we're hyper-focused on Japan. They're review aggregators, we're AI trip planners. They require constant internet, we work 100% offline.

**Q: Why Japan specifically? Why not global?**
A: Depth over breadth. Japan has unique challenges (language barrier, complex transit, cherry blossom timing). We solve those really well. Global expansion comes later.

**Q: What's your moat? Can this be easily copied?**
A: Data moat (500+ curated destinations, cherry blossom historical data), brand (first mover in this niche), and technical execution (offline-first is harder than it looks).

**Q: Are you profitable?**
A: Yes, from day 1. $2,500 MRR pre-launch, $47 in costs. Bootstrapped, no funding needed.

**Q: What's next after Japan?**
A: European destinations (Paris, Rome, Barcelona) then Asia expansion (Seoul, Bangkok, Taipei). But first, nail Japan.

**Q: How do you plan to grow?**
A: ProductHunt launch → SEO content → travel influencer partnerships → B2B (travel agencies). Organic, sustainable growth.

**Q: Why launch on ProductHunt?**
A: Community validation, initial traction, press exposure. It's the perfect platform for solo founders launching consumer SaaS.
