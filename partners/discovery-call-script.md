# Discovery Call Script - B2B Travel Agency Sales

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes before call)

- [ ] Review agency website and recent social media
- [ ] Check their current trip planning process (PDF? Email? Portal?)
- [ ] Note their specialties (cherry blossom, food tours, luxury, budget, etc.)
- [ ] Pull up demo link: `https://tripcompanion.app/partners/demo?agency={AgencyName}`
- [ ] Have pricing sheet ready
- [ ] Set up screen sharing

## Discovery Questions (5 minutes)

**Objective:** Understand their current pain points, booking process, and digital maturity

### Current Booking Process

> "Let me start by understanding how [Agency Name] works today. When a potential customer reaches out interested in a Japan trip, walk me through what happens."

**Listen for:**
- How they send itineraries (PDF, email, portal?)
- How many revisions they typically do
- How long from inquiry to booking
- What percentage of inquiries convert to bookings

**Probing questions:**

1. **"How do you currently send itineraries to prospective customers?"**
   - PDF? Email? Printed? Google Docs? Custom portal?

2. **"What's your typical conversion rate from inquiry to booking?"**
   - Industry standard is 15-25%
   - If they say "not sure" → highlight that as a tracking gap

3. **"What questions do customers ask most often after receiving an itinerary?"**
   - Listen for: "How do I get from X to Y?", "Where exactly is this restaurant?", "What if I get lost?"
   - These are MAP problems that our solution solves

4. **"How much time does your team spend on itinerary revisions and follow-up questions?"**
   - Quantify pain → position time savings

5. **"Do you currently embed affiliate links for JR Pass, tours, or restaurants in your itineraries?"**
   - If yes → "How much revenue does that generate?"
   - If no → "That's a missed revenue opportunity we can help with"

### Digital Strategy & Pain Points

> "Are you happy with your current itinerary delivery process, or is there room for improvement?"

**Listen for pain points:**
- "Customers get confused about directions"
- "We spend hours on PDF revisions"
- "People don't engage with our itineraries"
- "We're not making affiliate revenue"
- "Everything is manual"

**Key Insight to Extract:**
> **What is their #1 frustration with the current process?**

Write it down. You'll use this exact phrase later in the demo.

## Demo Walkthrough (5 minutes)

**Objective:** Show the platform solving their specific pain points

### Demo Setup

**[Share screen - open demo link]**

**2. Interactive Map (60 seconds) — THE KILLER FEATURE**

**This is the moment they lean in. Pause for reaction.**

**3. Multi-Language Toggle (20 seconds)**

> "Second thing — toggle between English, Japanese, and Chinese. Your customer's elderly mother who only speaks Chinese? She can read the entire itinerary in her language. One click."

**Demonstrate the language toggle in action**

**4. Offline Mode (20 seconds)**

**6. Real-Time Updates (20 seconds)**

## Objection Handling

### Common Objections & Responses

#### 1. "This seems expensive"

**Response:**
> "I totally understand. Let's look at it this way:
>
> If this helps you close just ONE extra $3,000 booking per month, you've paid for the annual contract 3x over. Based on our case studies, agencies see an average of 2.4 extra bookings per month.
>
> Plus the affiliate revenue is pure profit — $840/month on average. That alone covers more than half your cost.
>
> The question isn't really 'Is it expensive?' — it's 'Can you afford NOT to do this while your competitors are?'"

---

#### 2. "I need to think about it / discuss with my team"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely, makes sense. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the pricing, the features, or something else?
>
> **[If pricing]:** 'Let me ask — if the price were $0, would you sign up today?' [If yes → price objection is real, offer payment plan. If no → it's not really about price]
>
> **[If features]:** 'What features are you missing? Let me see if we have that or can build it.'
>
> **[If team buy-in]:** 'Who else needs to be involved in the decision? Can we schedule a follow-up call with them this week?'"

**IMPORTANT:** Don't let them leave without a clear next step.

> "How about this — let's schedule a 15-minute follow-up call for [specific day/time] where we can address any questions that come up. Does [day] at [time] work?"

---

#### 3. "We already have a solution"

**Response:**
> "That's great! What are you currently using?
>
> **[Listen to their answer]**
>
> How's that working for you? Are there any gaps or frustrations?
>
> **[Usually there are — dig into those]**
>
> The reason I ask is that most agencies we work with THOUGHT they had a solution, but it was missing [interactive maps / offline mode / affiliate revenue / multi-language]. Happy to show you a side-by-side comparison if that's helpful."

---

#### 4. "Can we start with a monthly plan instead of annual?"

**Response:**
> "We do offer monthly plans, but they're at full price — $[monthly price] per month.
>
> The annual contract gets you:
> - First month free ($[price] value)
> - Lower monthly rate
> - Priority support
> - Lock in current pricing (we're raising prices next quarter)
>
> The break-even is 3 months. Most agencies see ROI in the first 30 days. If you're confident this will work for [Agency Name] — and based on what you've told me about your volume, I'm confident it will — the annual makes way more sense."

---

#### 5. "We don't have the technical resources to implement this"

**Response:**
> "That's the beauty of this — you don't need technical resources. We handle everything:
>
> 1. **Setup:** We white-label it with your branding in 24 hours
> 2. **Training:** 1-hour onboarding call with your team
> 3. **Uploading trips:** You send us the itinerary (Word doc, PDF, whatever), we build it. Takes us 2 hours per trip.
> 4. **Ongoing support:** Email and chat support, response time under 4 hours
>
> Your team just needs to send us the itinerary and share the link with customers. That's it."

---

#### 6. "Our customers are older and not tech-savvy"

**Response:**
> "We hear this a lot, and it's actually the opposite. The people who MOST need this are non-tech-savvy travelers.
>
> Think about it: if you're 65 years old in Japan for the first time, what's easier:
>
> - Printing out a PDF, trying to read a tiny map, asking strangers for directions?
> - OR tapping your phone and seeing 'Walk this way for 12 minutes'?
>
> We've had customers in their 70s use this with zero issues. It's actually MORE user-friendly than a PDF because it's interactive. Plus, it works on any device — smartphone, tablet, even desktop before they leave."
